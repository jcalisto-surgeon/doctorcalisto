Frequently asked questions

Is robotic surgery safe for children?
Yes — in experienced hands and a high-volume program. The robot is a tool the surgeon controls completely; it adds magnified 3D vision and wristed instruments for finer, steadier movements. Dr. Calisto operates within one of the highest-volume pediatric robotic programs in the country and has published on its safe introduction in children.
What are the benefits compared with open surgery?
Robotic and minimally invasive surgery generally means smaller incisions, less pain, shorter hospital stays, and a faster return to normal activity — while preserving the precision needed for complex reconstruction.
What procedures can be done robotically?
A full spectrum — colorectal, gastrointestinal, and complex reconstructive procedures. Whether a robotic approach is right for your child depends on their specific condition and anatomy, which Dr. Calisto will review with you.
How do I know if my child is a candidate?
The best next step is a consultation. Dr. Calisto will review your child's diagnosis and imaging and recommend the approach — robotic, minimally invasive, or open — that gives the best result for your child.
